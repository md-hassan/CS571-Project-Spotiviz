Overview and Motivation

Music consumption has evolved significantly over time, influenced by cultural shifts, technological advancements, and platform-driven recommendations. Understanding these trends can provide valuable insights into how genre preferences, artist longevity, and album popularity change across different periods.

This project explores questions like: How long do viral songs or albums stay at the top? Do older tracks maintain relevance or experience sudden revivals? Which genres are rising or fading? How long do artists remain in the mainstream?

We also explore city-level genres analysis to uncover whether some genres are popular than others.

Key Insights from Our Visualizations

  • Artist chart domination is concentrated: Just 2,290 artists produced 5,981 charting songs ‚Äî a 2.6:1 ratio showing how success is skewed toward a few dominant artists.
  • Chart persistence varies: Most artists appear briefly, while only a handful show prolonged presence across years.
  • Genre dynamics are fluid: Timeline plots show Pop's consistent hold, while other genres rise and fall based on cultural trends.
  • Top charts are genre-concentrated: Pop, Rock, and Hip-Hop/Rap dominate global charts, leaving little space for Jazz, Reggae, Classical, etc.
  • City-level diversity reveals local tastes: Local Top 100 charts highlight emerging and regionally favored genres often missing from global rankings.
